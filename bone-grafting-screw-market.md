<p><p>The bone grafting screw market comprises three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws are known for their strength and durability, making them suitable for permanent applications. Titanium screws are lightweight, corrosion-resistant, and often preferred for their biocompatibility, promoting better integration with bone. Bioabsorbable screws are designed to gradually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for removal surgery, and are favored in specific applications for their ability to support healing while minimizing long-term foreign material presence.</p></p>
