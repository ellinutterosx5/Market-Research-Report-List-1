<p><p>The Paint Rheological Additives market is categorized into two primary types: inorganic and organic. Inorganic rheological additives, primarily composed of compounds like clay and silica, modify viscosity and improve stability in formulations. They enhance performance but may affect color and transparency. Organic rheological additives, derived from synthetic or natural polymers, offer superior clarity and adaptability in various applications. They are commonly used to achieve desired flow characteristics and stability in water-based and solvent-based paints, influencing the overall finish and application ease.</p></p>
