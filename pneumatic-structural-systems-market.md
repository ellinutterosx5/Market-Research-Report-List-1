<p><p>Pneumatic structural systems utilize air pressure to maintain their shape and stability, offering lightweight, modular solutions for various applications. Air-supported structures are typically large, temporary structures, relying on constant air pressure to remain inflated, making them ideal for events or warehouses. Conversely, air-inflated structures have a more rigid design, with air-filled elements providing strength and form, suited for long-term installations like sports facilities and commercial buildings. Both types leverage efficiency and versatility for modern architectural needs.</p></p>
