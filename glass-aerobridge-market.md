The electromechanical elevation system utilizes electric motors and gear-driven mechanisms to facilitate smooth and efficient movement of the bridge. In contrast, the hydraulic elevation system relies on hydraulic fluid and cylinders to lift and lower the bridge, offering greater power and stability. Both systems serve to enhance passenger boarding experiences and improve operational efficiency at airports while ensuring safety and reliability in various environmental conditions.</p></p>
